Nit

The head louse is an obligate ectoparasite of humans. Head lice are wingless insects that spend their entire lives on the human scalp and feeding exclusively on human blood. Humans are the only known hosts of this specific parasite, while chimpanzees host a closely related species, Pediculus schaeffi

Psocodea

Adult alderflies are usually black, dark brown, or gray. They look a lot like stoneflies but are more closely related to fishflies and dobsonflies. They are generally poor fliers. At rest, the 2 pairs of heavily veined wings are held folded over the back in a rooflike (usually not flat) shape.
